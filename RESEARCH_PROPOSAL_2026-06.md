## Rationale

Three concrete shifts in Senior AI Infrastructure Engineer postings observed during 2026-03-01 to 2026-06-01 drive these additive proposals. (1) LLM-as-judge has crossed from research curiosity to production CI gate: postings from Reddit, Airbnb, Zoom, GE Vernova, and Affinity now ask for evaluation pipelines, calibration vs human labels, and regression-on-merge — none of which is covered in the current mod-206 syllabus that stops at MLflow/drift detection. (2) Agentic systems infrastructure has matured into a distinct line item with Temporal, multi-agent coordination, and AIOps-style auto-remediation appearing as senior-level requirements at Scale AI Enterprise (2026-06-01), Justworks (2026-06-01), Synthesia (2026-05), Affinity (2026-06-01), Airbnb (2026-05), and Recursion (2026-05) — current curriculum has no module that owns the operational concerns of agents in production (durable workflows, state management, tool invocation reliability, observability over agent traces). (3) HPC/Slurm has not gone away — postings from Wex (2026-02-12), Centific (2026-05), Perplexity (2026-04-07), and Cedana (2026-04) all explicitly require Slurm administration and Slurm/K8s bridging (Kueue, multi-cluster Slurm federation); mod-201 covers gang scheduling and K8s-native batch but does not address Slurm interop, which is now table-stakes for any AI infra role that touches large training clusters. All three additions are strictly additive, target clear gaps the senior level genuinely owns, and each is backed by 3+ distinct in-window postings. Items found in postings but already covered (vLLM/KV cache in mod-204, MIG/DCGM in mod-203, ArgoCD in mod-208, multi-cloud IaC in mod-205/208) are NOT proposed. Items that belong at a different level per the ownership rule (LLM/AI security → ai-infra-security-learning; HPC networking depth → ai-infra-performance-learning; vector DBs and prompt engineering → ai-infra-engineer-learning module 10) are NOT proposed here; JOB_REQUIREMENTS.md links to those owners instead.
