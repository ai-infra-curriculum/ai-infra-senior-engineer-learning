# Interview Readiness Guide – AI Infrastructure Senior Engineer

## Purpose
Help you transform senior-level modules into interview-ready narratives focused on reliability, drift management, and strategic partnership.

## Core Competencies to Highlight
- Preventing and resolving infrastructure drift  
- Designing observability systems with actionable SLOs  
- Leading incident response and postmortem improvements  
- Influencing product direction through infrastructure insight

## Preparation Checklist
1. Select one story per competency (drift prevention, observability, incident leadership).  
2. Collect artefacts such as incident timelines, SLO dashboards, and runbooks.  
3. Practice articulating trade-offs, risk assessments, and cross-functional collaboration.  
4. Outline how you measure success for infra initiatives beyond uptime.

## Mock Interview Structure (50 minutes)
| Segment | Objective | Repo Resource |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 5 min | Career context | Root README |
| 15 min | Drift or observability deep dive | `lessons/mod-002` / `mod-003` |
| 15 min | Incident leadership walkthrough | `projects/project-02` |
| 10 min | Mentorship / partnership discussion | Team collaboration notes |
| 5 min | Candidate questions | See below |

## Sample Technical Prompts
- “Walk through how you detect and remediate drift in a distributed environment.”  
- “How do you ensure SLOs remain meaningful as systems evolve?”  
- “Describe a major incident you led—what changed afterwards?”

## Behavioral Prompts
- Handling disagreement with stakeholders during an outage  
- Coaching teammates through complex investigations  
- Balancing speed with safety during production changes

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer
- “How do senior infra engineers partner with product and data leadership?”  
- “What metrics define success for your reliability programme?”  
- “How does your team share incident learnings across the organisation?”
